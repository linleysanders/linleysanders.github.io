About Me

I'm a graduate from Columbia University's Graduate School of Journalism in the Lede Program for data journalism. If you click around, you'll find a published data visualization articles and in-the-works projects I've built to expand my knowledge of Python, R, PostgreSQL, interactive graphics, web scraping, data cleaning, and basic statistical analysis. By trade, I'm a journalist who decided coding could make me a better reporter. I've worked for ProPublica's Electionland, The Associated Press, Newsweek and The Kansas City Star. My passion for data-driven news and women's political interests carried me from Kansas to Iowa to New York City. I'm currently a contributing news reporter for TeenVogue.com, where I've written over 175 articles to help women engage with daily news, politics, and health.
